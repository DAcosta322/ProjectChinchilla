Analysis of Submission 54133 — TOMATOES Shorting Failure
The Price Drop (ts 0–50000)
The TOMATOES mid price dropped from ~5006 (ts=0) down to ~4982 (ts=45000) 
— a decline of about 24 points. This is the opportunity you noticed.

What Went Wrong: The Algo Went Long Instead of Short
Looking at the position reconstruction, the algo briefly went short early on 
(reaching -10 at ts=48700), but then reversed into a massive long — buying 11 at ts=51500, 
and continuing to pile on buys until it hit +80 (the position limit!) by ts=87100/108300.

The root cause is the slow MA (SLOW_WINDOW = 500). Here's the problem:

During the first 500 ticks (~50,000 timestamps), compute_fair_value just returns 
the current mid price (line 127: return mid). There's no momentum signal 
— the algo is essentially blind to the trend during the entire warm-up period.

Once the slow MA kicks in (~ts 50000+), it's lagging badly. The slow MA is an average 
of the last 500 mid prices, which includes the higher prices from early on. 
So the slow MA sits above the current price. The fast MA (8 ticks) tracks current prices more closely. 
This means fast_ma - slow_ma is negative — the signal says "momentum is down." 
But the fair value formula is slow_ma + signal * 0.5, which pulls fair value down even further below the market. 
This makes the algo think everything at the current ask price is expensive, so it won't sell/short.

The resting orders compound the problem. 
The algo posts buy orders at fair_value - 2 and sell orders at fair_value + 2. 
Since the computed fair value is dragged below the market by the lagging slow MA + negative signal, 
the buy orders are close to the market (getting filled constantly), 
while the sell orders sit too far below the actual market to attract any counterparty.

The Net Effect
ts 0–18800: Small short (-9 max) during the warm-up period (using raw mid as fair value — correct behavior by accident)
ts 24600–50000: Starts flipping between short and long as mid price fluctuates
ts 51500 onward: The slow MA activates with a stale high average, fair value gets distorted downward, 
and the algo accumulates a huge long position (up to +80) during the drop — the exact opposite of what you wanted
The PNL story confirms this
The TOMATOES PNL goes negative around ts=89000–93600 (dropping to about -250), 
exactly when the algo is sitting at position +70 while prices bottom out. It recovers only because prices eventually stabilize/bounce.

Summary
The dual-MA strategy cannot short a downtrend because:

The 500-tick warm-up period means no signal during the initial drop
Once active, the lagging slow MA creates a fair value below the market, causing the algo to buy (not sell)
The position quickly maxes out long (+80), trapping the algo on the wrong side
Want to discuss fixes — like shortening the warm-up, using a different signal for trend-following, 
or adding a dedicated shorting mode when momentum is clearly negative?
